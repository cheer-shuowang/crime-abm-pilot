# Crime ABM Pilot: LLM-Driven Community Deliberation Study

A pilot implementation of an agent-based model (ABM) using Large Language Models (LLMs) to simulate community deliberation and collective efficacy formation across different cultural contexts.

## Project Overview

This project explores how cultural composition (individualism vs. collectivism) and institutional communication strategies shape collective efficacy through community deliberation. Rather than hard-coding cultural differences as parameters, we use LLM-driven agents to generate emergent collective behavior.

**Research Questions:**
- How do collectivist vs. individualist communities respond differently to governance failures?
- What role does "silence" (self-censorship) play in eroding or maintaining collective efficacy?
- How does institutional communication strategy (procedural justice vs. blame-shifting) interact with cultural context?

**Core Innovation:**
- Uses Theory of Mind-based internal simulation and multi-agent deliberation to model cultural differences
- Avoids circular reasoning of traditional parameter-based cultural modeling
- Generates emergent collective efficacy patterns from agent interactions

## Current Status

**Phase 1: Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** - In Progress

Testing core functionality:
- LLM-powered agents with cultural personas
- Community deliberation simulation
- Silence pattern measurement (attendance-speech gap, opinion gap)
- Cost tracking and logging
- Quality validation (pending)

**Planned Next Steps:**
- Phase 2: Quality testing with 10 agents and human review of deliberation content
- Phase 3: Multi-condition comparison (Culture × Institution Response, 2×2 design)
- Full Experiment: 30 agents × 500 time steps × 8 conditions × 50 replications
